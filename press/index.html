The story

Why meatballs? Because they exist everywhere and belong somewhere. A shape that travels across continents, adapts to local kitchens, yet stays recognisable. From Albanian qofte to Swedish köttbullar, from Chinese lion's head to Mexican albóndigas — each version carries generations of technique and tradition.

This is not a recipe app. It is a reference library built like a small magazine. Each entry is a cultural portrait: where the dish belongs, why it matters, how it came to be. The cooking instructions exist, but context comes first.

Meatballs is an independent project based in Stockholm. No algorithms, no user-generated content, no trends. Just careful documentation of dishes worth knowing.

Food apps have become content factories. We wanted the opposite — a book that happens to be an app. Notes matter. History matters.

For journalists

Meatballs is designed for readers, not users. The app prioritises typography, pacing, and depth over engagement metrics. Every entry is written in British English with attention to voice and accuracy.

If you're writing about food culture, design, or independent apps, we're happy to provide additional context, quotes, or assets. Response time is usually within 24 hours.
